# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Professionals working at a desk with a phone mounted in landscape orientation as a secondary dock display. They glance at it for quick information without picking up the phone. Context includes home office and co-working spaces where the phone sits beside a laptop or monitor.

## Product Purpose

A minimalist productivity dashboard that streamlines the working routine by providing at-a-glance information and quick actions. Weather, calendar, music playback, voice agent, pomodoro timer, smart home controls, and portfolio overview — all accessible with zero to one taps. The dock is passive by default, active on demand.

## Brand Personality

Minimalist, Calm, Functional

The interface stays out of the way. Dark background, restrained use of color, clear typographic hierarchy. No decorative flourishes. Every element earns its place by being useful at a glance from arm's length.

## Anti-references

No strong anti-references. Avoid generic SaaS dashboard patterns (heavy cards, side-stripe accents, hero metrics). Avoid anything that feels like it belongs on a phone home screen — this is a companion display, not a phone app.

## Design Principles

1. **Glanceable, not engaging** — Information resolves in under a second. The dock is read at a distance; text is large, contrast is high, hierarchy is immediate. No element requires scrutiny.

2. **Frictionless actions** — Every interactive path is 1-2 taps. The dock serves someone mid-workflow who needs weather, a timer start, a skip track, or a light toggle without breaking focus.

3. **Ambient awareness** — Information is present but not demanding. Polling is silent, updates are smooth, nothing flashes or animates unnecessarily. The user should never feel interrupted by their own dashboard.

4. **Calm technology** — Dark theme, low chroma neutrals, one green accent (Spotify). Visual hierarchy through luminance contrast rather than color. The interface recedes; the information persists.

5. **Landscape-first integrity** — Designed for 667 x 375 px viewport with safe-area awareness. Every panel is exactly one viewport wide. No scrolling within panels, no ambiguous tap targets at the edges.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Standard WCAG AA contrast. Touch targets respect minimum sizing at 667px width. Font sizes use clamped values that scale with viewport. Safe areas respected for notched devices.
